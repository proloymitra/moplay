Responsible Gaming

Gaming should be fun, safe, and enjoyable for everyone

Our Commitment

At moplay, we believe gaming should be an enjoyable and positive experience for all our users. We are committed to promoting responsible gaming practices and providing tools to help maintain a healthy relationship with gaming.

While our games are free-to-play and designed for entertainment, we recognize the importance of helping users maintain balance in their gaming habits and overall well-being.

What is Healthy Gaming?

Time Management

Set reasonable time limits for gaming sessions and take regular breaks to rest your eyes and stretch.

Balance

Maintain a healthy balance between gaming and other important activities like work, school, exercise, and socializing.

Emotional Well-being

Gaming should enhance your mood and provide relaxation, not cause stress, anxiety, or negative emotions.

Social Connection

Use gaming as a way to connect with others, but don't let it replace face-to-face social interactions.

Recognizing Warning Signs

Be aware of these signs that gaming might be becoming problematic:

Time-Related Signs

  • Gaming longer than intended
  • Losing track of time
  • Gaming late into night
  • Constantly thinking about games

Social & Personal Signs

  • Neglecting responsibilities
  • Avoiding social activities
  • Lying about gaming time
  • Irritability when unable to game

Physical & Mental Signs

  • Eye strain, headaches
  • Changes in sleep patterns
  • Anxiety when not gaming
  • Gaming to escape problems

Our Responsible Gaming Tools

Session Timers

Set gaming session time limits and receive reminders when it's time to take a break.

Play Time Tracking

Monitor your daily and weekly gaming time to maintain awareness of your habits.

Break Reminders

Receive periodic reminders to take breaks, stretch, and rest your eyes.

Self-Exclusion

Temporarily restrict your access to games if you need a break from gaming.

Healthy Gaming Guidelines

Before You Start Gaming

  • Set a time limit for your session
  • Complete important tasks first
  • Ensure comfortable environment
  • Have water and snacks nearby

During Gaming

  • Take breaks every hour
  • Stay hydrated and maintain posture
  • Be mindful of your feelings
  • Don't game when angry or upset

After Gaming

  • Reflect on how the session made you feel
  • Engage in other activities you enjoy
  • Maintain social connections offline
  • Get adequate sleep and exercise

For Parents

  • Monitor your child's gaming time
  • Encourage other activities
  • Play games together as family
  • Discuss online safety

Getting Help

If you or someone you know is struggling with gaming habits, help is available

Professional Support

  • Mental health professional
  • Healthcare provider
  • Counselor or therapist

Support Organizations

  • Gaming Disorders Support
  • SAMHSA (US)
  • Mind (UK)

Crisis Support

  • Suicide Prevention: 988 (US)
  • Samaritans: 116 123 (UK)
  • Crisis Text: HOME to 741741
